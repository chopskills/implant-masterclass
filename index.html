Frequently Asked Questions

Questions we get every time.

If something isn't here, just send us a WhatsApp — we respond personally.

Do I need prior implant experience to attend?+
No prior implant experience is required. The course is designed for general dentists who have never placed an implant. If you have placed implants before, you will still benefit from the live surgery, the prosthetic workflow, and the ongoing mentorship — but the course is built to take someone from zero to confident placement.
Will I actually place an implant on a real patient?+
Yes. Day 2 is live surgery. You will place one real implant on a real, consented patient, with Dr. Narasimman standing beside you for every step. This is not a simulation, not a pig jaw, not a model. You also take home one implant to use at your own clinic.
What is the in-clinic mentorship visit?+
Within 5 months of your implant placement date, our team visits your clinic — wherever you practise in India — and guides your first fully independent case, in your own setup, on your own patient, step by step. This is included in Course B at no additional charge. No other implant course in India currently offers this.
What are the online sessions after the course?+
Starting from the day of your implant placement, we run live online sessions every 2 weeks for 3 months — a structured 6-session programme. Every session is clinic-centric: you bring your real cases, your real doubts, your complications. Dr. Narasimman addresses them live. No rigid syllabus — we address what you actually face in your practice. Goal: fully independent before the 5-month in-clinic visit.
Where is the course held?+
The onsite workshop is held in Selaiyur, Chennai, on 4 & 5 July 2026. The online mentorship sessions (every 2 weeks for 3 months) are held virtually. The in-clinic mentorship visit happens at your own clinic, wherever you practise in India.
Why only 5 seats?+
Because every participant places a real implant on Day 2. That requires individual surgical supervision from Dr. Narasimman. More than 5 participants would compromise the quality of that supervision. The small batch is a feature, not a limitation — it's why the course works.
